# PLAN: Comprehensive Narrative Review — The Remnant

_Created: 2026-04-01 | Type: Editorial audit (all narrative content)_

## Objective

Perform a professional editorial review of every room, NPC, dialogue tree, quest, and world event in The Remnant against the canonical narrative bibles. Identify inconsistencies, weak writing, broken story logic, missing connections, tone violations, and progression gaps. Produce a prioritized fix list.

## Ground Truth Documents
- `content/the-remnant-narrative-bible.md` — 3-act structure, central mystery, thematic pillars
- `content/the-remnant-world-bible.md` — Factions, geography, tone, voice rules
- `content/the-remnant-MASTER-GAME-BIBLE.md` — Consolidated reference
- `content/the-remnant-death-regeneration.md` — Permadeath, revenant, cycle system
- `content/the-remnant-dungeon-script.md` — Room-by-room walkthrough

## Review Dimensions (Applied to Every Room)

1. **Narrative Consistency** — Does the room's story match canonical facts? Timeline, faction beliefs, CHARON-7 science, geography
2. **Tone & Voice** — Does the prose follow the writing rules? Terse under tension, lyrical in quiet, no fantasy language, second-person present, sensory detail
3. **Story Progression** — Does this room advance the player's understanding appropriately for its act/zone? No premature reveals, no dead-end lore
4. **World Coherence** — Do NPCs, items, enemies, and environmental details make sense for this location?
5. **Decision Realism** — Are player choices and their consequences believable? Do faction reactions make sense?
6. **Interconnection** — Do exits, narrative keys, quest hooks, and dialogue references connect properly to other rooms/zones?
7. **Thematic Alignment** — Does this room serve at least one of the five pillars? (Humanity != biology, scarcity reveals character, memory is burden, monster isn't obvious, hope is defiance)
8. **Writing Quality** — Prose quality, cliche avoidance, sensory richness, atmosphere, pacing

## Decomposition (6 Howlers)

### howler-act1-origins (38 rooms)
**Zones**: Crossroads (15) + River Road (23)
First impression zones. Must orient without overwhelming, introduce factions, establish tone, create the hook.

### howler-human-factions (68 rooms)
**Zones**: Covenant (28) + Salt Creek (20) + The Ember (20)
Three major human faction bases. Must present each philosophy authentically, show strengths AND ugliness.

### howler-wilderness (58 rooms)
**Zones**: The Breaks (20) + The Dust (18) + The Pine Sea (20)
Wild/transition zones. Must maintain atmosphere through environmental storytelling without NPCs carrying it.

### howler-dark-factions (56 rooms)
**Zones**: The Stacks (20) + Duskhollow (18) + The Pens (18)
Reclaimers, Covenant of Dusk, Red Court. Must handle Sanguine with nuance — people, not monsters.

### howler-endgame (48 rooms)
**Zones**: The Deep (20) + The Scar (28)
The horror gauntlet and truth reveal. Carries the entire narrative payoff. All four endings must land.

### howler-systems (cross-cutting)
**Scope**: 21 dialogue trees, 125 NPCs, 127+ quests, world events, narrative keys, companion narration
Cross-zone consistency. Dialogue-to-room alignment. Quest completability. NPC placement coherence.

## Output

- Executive summary of narrative health
- Critical issues (story-breaking)
- Major issues (immersion-breaking)
- Minor issues (polish)
- Room-by-room notes
- Cross-zone connection audit
- Prioritized fix recommendations
