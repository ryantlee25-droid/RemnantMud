# THE REMNANT — World Bible & Game Design Reference

> A post-apocalyptic MUD set in a world where a weaponized pathogen collapsed civilization and split humanity into three factions: the living, the turned, and the things that feed on both.

---

## CORE PREMISE

### The Collapse

In 2031, a gene-editing pathogen designated **CHARON-7** escaped from a joint military-pharmaceutical black site buried in the Colorado Rockies. Originally designed as a battlefield incapacitant — a virus that would suppress aggression and higher cognition in enemy combatants — CHARON-7 mutated during an unauthorized live trial on death row inmates. The mutation inverted its purpose. Instead of suppressing aggression, it rewired the limbic system into a permanent state of predatory hunger while degrading the prefrontal cortex to nothing.

Within six weeks, 60% of the global population was infected. The infected — called **the Hollow** — are not zombies in the classical sense. They retain fragments of motor memory, sometimes speech, sometimes muscle memory of their former lives. A Hollow that was once a mechanic might still fumble with wrenches. A Hollow soldier might still shoulder a rifle, though it can't aim. This makes them unpredictable and deeply unsettling.

### The Second Horror

CHARON-7 had a second mutation no one anticipated. In roughly 1 in 10,000 infected, the virus didn't degrade the brain — it *restructured* it. These individuals didn't become Hollow. They became something older. Something that mythology had already named.

They are called **the Sanguine** — apex predators with enhanced senses, inhuman speed, and a metabolic dependency on human blood. They are not supernatural. They are the virus's masterpiece: a perfectly adapted predator sitting atop a collapsing food chain. They are intelligent, organized, and patient. Some remember their former lives completely. Some wish they didn't.

The Sanguine are not unified. They have factions, politics, and philosophical divides about whether to farm humans, protect them, or simply rule them.

### The Living

Scattered across the bones of the old world, **the Unturned** survive in fortified settlements, scavenger bands, and underground bunkers. Some have learned to coexist (or negotiate) with Sanguine enclaves. Others shoot anything that isn't verifiably human on sight. Trust is the scarcest resource on earth.

It is now **2038**. Seven years after the Collapse.

## TONE & ATMOSPHERE

### Guiding Principles

- **Dread over gore.** The scariest moments are quiet. A child's shoe in an empty school. Radio static that almost sounds like words. The feeling of being watched from a treeline at dusk.
- **Moral ambiguity over good vs. evil.** The Sanguine aren't villains by default. The Unturned aren't heroes by default. Survival makes everyone compromise.
- **Scarcity is king.** Bullets, clean water, medicine, trust — everything is rationed. Abundance is suspicious.
- **The world is beautiful and broken.** Nature is reclaiming everything. Wildflowers push through highway asphalt. Elk herds move through abandoned suburbs. The apocalypse is gorgeous when nothing is trying to kill you.
- **Folklore is forming.** Seven years in, people are already creating myths, religions, and superstitions about the Collapse. Truth is becoming legend.

### Writing Voice

- Terse. Declarative. Short sentences when tension is high.
- Lyrical when describing landscape or quiet moments.
- Never clinical or detached — this world has a pulse.
- Dark humor is welcome. Gallows wit. Survivors laugh or they break.
- No "thee/thou" fantasy language. This is a world that had iPhones seven years ago.

### Color Palette (for room descriptions)

- **Dawn/Dusk:** Amber, rust, bruised purple, copper light
- **Midday:** Washed out, bleached, too-bright silence
- **Night:** Ink black, blue-gray, silver where moonlight hits
- **Underground:** Sulfur yellow (lanterns), deep shadow, wet stone
- **Sanguine spaces:** Candlelight, velvet dark, old wood, the smell of iron

## FACTIONS

### The Unturned (Playable)

Baseline humans. No enhancements, no infection. What you see is what you get.

**Subfactions:**

| Name | Philosophy | Base of Operations | Disposition |
|------|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **The Accord** | Rebuild civilization through cooperation and law | Covenant (former courthouse complex) | Cautiously diplomatic |
| **The Salters** | Survival through strength; might makes right | Salt Creek Stronghold | Aggressive, territorial |
| **The Drifters** | No allegiance, no roots; trade and move | Mobile caravans | Neutral, opportunistic |
| **The Kindling** | Believe the Collapse was divine punishment; seek redemption | The Ember (a converted church) | Zealous, unpredictable |
| **The Reclaimers** | Scavenge and reverse-engineer old-world technology | The Stacks (a ruined data center) | Isolationist, pragmatic |

### The Sanguine (Playable — Advanced)

CHARON-7 restructured. Apex predators with a blood dependency.

**Traits:**
- Enhanced senses (hearing, smell, night vision)
- Increased speed and strength
- Accelerated healing (not instant — hours, not seconds)
- Photosensitivity (daylight causes pain and debuff, not instant death)
- Must consume human blood regularly or suffer cognitive and physical degradation
- Lifespan unknown — oldest known Sanguine is only 7 years turned

**Subfactions:**

| Name | Philosophy | Territory | Notes |
|------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **The Covenant of Dusk** | Coexist with humans through blood tithes and mutual protection | Duskhollow Manor | The "civilized" option. Controversial among both species. |
| **The Red Court** | Humans are livestock. Dominion is natural law. | The Pens (a converted livestock facility) | Ruthless, hierarchical, effective |
| **The Ferals** | Reject structure. Hunt. Feed. Move. | Nomadic, wilderness | Closest to pure predator. Avoid other Sanguine. |
| **The Lucid** | Retain their human identity. Seek a cure. Drink animal blood (poorly nourishing). | Scattered, often hiding among Unturned | Tragic figures. Constantly hungry. |

### The Hollow (NPC / Enemy)

The infected masses. Not playable. Range from shambling and broken to terrifyingly competent.

**Hollow Types:**

| Type | Description | Threat Level |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Shuffler** | Classic slow infected. Degraded motor function. Travels in herds. | Low (individually), High (in numbers) |
| **Remnant** | Retains fragments of former skill. A Remnant cop might still draw a weapon. Unpredictable. | Medium |
| **Screamer** | Emits a piercing vocalization that draws other Hollow from a wide radius. | Medium (but force multiplier) |
| **Brute** | Muscle hypertrophy from the virus. Larger, stronger, slower to react but devastating on contact. | High |
| **Whisperer** | Rare. Retains partial speech. Will mimic phrases, names, even conversations to lure prey. | Very High (psychological threat) |
| **Hive Mother** | Extremely rare. Seems to exert pheromonal control over nearby Hollow. Always surrounded. | Extreme |

## GEOGRAPHY — THE REMNANT MAP

The game world is set in the **Four Corners region** of the American Southwest — where Colorado, New Mexico, Utah, and Arizona meet. High desert, deep canyons, mountain passes, and scattered small towns connected by crumbling highways.

### Major Settlements

| Name | Type | Location Inspiration | Description |
|------|------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Covenant** | Unturned city (The Accord) | Durango, CO | The closest thing to civilization. Built around a fortified courthouse and surrounding blocks. Running water from the Animas River. Population ~800. |
| **Salt Creek Stronghold** | Unturned fortress (The Salters) | Farmington, NM | A walled compound built from shipping containers and earthworks. Militaristic. Population ~300. |
| **Duskhollow Manor** | Sanguine enclave (Covenant of Dusk) | Converted ranch estate, Mancos Valley | A sprawling estate where Sanguine and willing human "tithers" coexist. Eerily beautiful. Candlelit. |
| **The Stacks** | Unturned tech outpost (Reclaimers) | Abandoned data center near Cortez, CO | Partially powered by solar arrays. The closest thing to an internet that still exists — local mesh network. |
| **The Ember** | Religious settlement (The Kindling) | Converted cathedral, Pagosa Springs | Fire motifs everywhere. The Kindling believe purification through flame cleanses CHARON-7. They're not entirely wrong about fire. |
| **The Pens** | Sanguine stronghold (Red Court) | Converted feedlot, south of Shiprock, NM | Humans kept as blood stock. The darkest place on the map. Rescuing captives from here is endgame content. |
| **Crossroads** | Neutral trading post (Drifters) | Junction of US-160 and US-550 | Open market. No faction warfare permitted inside the perimeter. Enforced by Drifter arbiters with shotguns and reputation. |

### Wilderness Zones

| Zone | Terrain | Threat Level | Notes |
|------|---------|-------------|-------|
| **The Scar** | Blast crater and surrounding deadlands | Extreme | Where CHARON-7 originated. Rumored to still contain military-grade tech and bioweapon samples. Heavily irradiated? Or is that just a story to keep people away? |
| **The Breaks** | Canyon lands, narrow passages, slot canyons | High | Perfect ambush territory. Ferals hunt here. Beautiful and lethal. |
| **The Pine Sea** | Dense mountain forest, 8,000+ ft elevation | Medium | Hollow thin out at altitude (cold slows them). Good hunting, foraging. Sanguine avoid — too exposed in daylight hours. |
| **The Dust** | High desert flats, ruins of small towns | Medium | Long sightlines, brutal heat during the day. Hollow herds migrate through seasonally. |
| **The River Road** | Along the Animas/San Juan river corridor | Variable | The lifeline. Settlements cluster along water. Also where conflict concentrates. |
| **The Deep** | Abandoned mine networks | High | Completely dark. Hollow nest here. Also rumored Sanguine lairs. Rich in salvageable minerals and old-world equipment. |

## NAMING CONVENTIONS

### Character Names (Human/Unturned)

People in The Remnant use names that feel like 2031 was seven years ago — because it was. Modern first names are common. Surnames are sometimes dropped in favor of descriptors.

**Examples:**
- Marcus Cole
- Elena "Wires" Vasquez (Reclaimer engineer)
- Deacon Harrow (Kindling priest)
- Tomás Redfern
- Kira Blackwood
- "Patch" (no other name given — Drifter medic)
- Juno Alspaugh
- Silas Crain
- Maren Duvall
- Cal "Dustmouth" Price

**Naming patterns:**
- First + Last is standard for established settlement residents
- Nickname only is common for Drifters and loners
- Title + Last Name for authority figures (Deacon, Marshal, Elder)
- Callsigns for Salter military types (Anvil, Reaper, Foxhole)

### Character Names (Sanguine)

Sanguine often adopt new names after turning — a psychological break from their former lives.

**Examples:**
- Vesper (Covenant of Dusk leader)
- Silas Wren → now just "The Wren" (Red Court enforcer)
- Dr. Ama Osei → still uses her full name (Lucid researcher)
- Moth (Feral, named by Unturned who've spotted her)
- Castellan Rook (Red Court, title + chosen name)
- Lachlan Gray (Covenant of Dusk, kept his human name)
- "The Pale" (Feral legend — may not be real)

**Naming patterns:**
- Covenant of Dusk: Often keep human names or adopt elegant/archaic ones
- Red Court: Titles + chosen names (Castellan, Warden, Reeve)
- Ferals: Named by others, usually descriptive (Moth, Ghost, Bone)
- Lucid: Keep their human names as an act of defiance

### Town/Location Names

Names should feel like they evolved organically — some are old names repurposed, some are new.

**Patterns:**
- Old town names that stuck: Cortez, Durango, Pagosa
- Renamed by new inhabitants: Covenant, The Stacks, Duskhollow
- Descriptive geography: Salt Creek, The Breaks, The Dust
- Dark humor or grim poetry: The Pens, The Scar, Bone Hollow
- Drifter slang for waypoints: "Click 9" (9 miles from Crossroads), "The Dry" (waterless stretch)

## GAME MECHANICS NOTES

### Time of Day Matters

The game runs on a day/night cycle. Time of day fundamentally changes gameplay:

- **Dawn/Dusk:** Transition periods. Most faction activity. Trading, missions, social interaction.
- **Midday:** Safest from Sanguine. Hollow are active but sluggish in heat. Best time for open-ground travel.
- **Night:** Sanguine at full power. Hollow more active (cooler temperatures). Travel is dangerous. Indoor/settlement activities peak.
- **Deep Night (2 AM - 4 AM):** Highest danger. Hollow herds move. Sanguine hunt. Only Salter patrols and Drifter night-runners are out.

### Infection System

CHARON-7 is everywhere. Every human character has an **Infection Resistance** stat. Certain events increase exposure:

- Hollow bite or scratch (major exposure)
- Consuming contaminated water/food (moderate exposure)
- Prolonged time in The Scar (cumulative exposure)
- Blood contact with Sanguine (minor but nonzero exposure)

If Infection Resistance fails, the character doesn't immediately turn. There's a **degradation period** — increasing debuffs, hallucinations, personality shifts in roleplay text — before the final turn. This period can be slowed (not stopped) with medicine. Some quests involve the desperate search for a way to reverse early-stage infection.

**Permadeath option:** Players can opt into permadeath mode where a full infection = character death. Non-permadeath characters are "rescued" and wake up at a settlement with severe stat penalties.

### Reputation System

Every faction tracks the player's standing independently:

| Level | Name | Effect |
|-------|------|--------|
| -3 | **Hunted** | Faction members attack on sight |
| -2 | **Hostile** | Denied entry. May be ambushed. |
| -1 | **Distrusted** | Limited access. Higher prices. Watched. |
| 0 | **Unknown** | Neutral. Must prove yourself. |
| +1 | **Recognized** | Basic trade and services available |
| +2 | **Trusted** | Full access. Faction missions available. |
| +3 | **Blooded** | Inner circle. Faction leadership quests. Unique gear. |

Raising reputation with one faction may lower it with rivals (e.g., helping The Accord may anger The Salters).

### Crafting System

Crafting is central to survival. Items are built from **components** found through scavenging:

**Component Categories:**
- **Scrap Metal** — weapon and armor crafting
- **Textiles** — clothing, bandages, rope
- **Electronics** — radios, lights, traps
- **Chemicals** — medicine, fire paste, explosives
- **Organics** — food, herbs, poisons
- **Rare Parts** — pre-Collapse tech, military components, medical equipment

Crafting requires both the right components AND sufficient skill level. Recipes can be found, taught by NPCs, or experimented into (with risk of wasting materials).

## POST-COLLAPSE DEMOGRAPHICS — THE CHILDREN

It is 2038. The Collapse happened in 2031. Seven years of births means the Four Corners has a generation of children between zero and seven years old who have never known the old world.

This is not a detail. It is a fracture line running through every settlement.

**Who they are:**
Children born after 2031 have no memory of grocery stores, hospitals, school buses, or safe nights. They don't grieve the old world because they never had it. The apocalypse isn't a wound for them — it's the weather. Hollow are just a thing that happens. Blood tithes are just a thing some families do. Drifters are just people.

**Why this matters narratively:**
Adults in The Remnant are haunted by contrast — they know what was lost. Post-Collapse children have no contrast. Their relationship to danger is pragmatic in a way that disturbs their parents. A seven-year-old at Covenant who calmly reports three Hollow at the south fence, eats breakfast, and then asks if they can go play — that child is not broken. That child is adapted. That's either the most hopeful thing in the game or the most terrifying, depending on how you look at it.

**How they appear in the world:**
Post-Collapse children should appear in settlements as NPCs and environmental detail — not as quest objects or cuteness props. They are present the way children in any village are present: underfoot, watchful, competent at the things their world demands.

Design examples:
- A girl at Covenant, roughly six years old, who can name every Hollow type by the sound they make. She's never been outside the walls.
- Boys at Salt Creek who run Hollow-counting games during patrols, keeping tally on a stick. The older Salters pretend not to notice.
- A three-year-old at Elder Kai Nez's canyon settlement who was born after the Collapse. He has never heard silence — the canyon wind is just what air sounds like.
- Children in The Ember settlement who know Harrow's purification chants before they know nursery rhymes, because that's what the adults sang.

**The fracture:**
Adults sometimes can't look directly at post-Collapse children. Not because they're threatening — because they're fine. Because a child laughing at the base of a watchtower while Hollow howl in the distance is proof that this is normal now. That the world the adults mourned is gone in a way that doesn't get better. The grief for the old world dies with the generation that knew it. These children are the proof.

**Writing rule:** Post-Collapse children should appear in at least two room descriptions per major settlement zone. They are never the focus — they're present the way furniture is present. Their competence and adaptation are the horror. Their joy, when it appears, is the hope.

## QUEST THEMES

Quests should generally fall into these categories:

1. **Survival** — Find water, food, medicine, shelter. The basics never stop mattering.
2. **Faction Politics** — Negotiate, spy, sabotage, ally-build. Who you help shapes the world.
3. **Mystery** — What really happened at the CHARON-7 site? Are there other labs? Is a cure possible?
4. **Rescue** — People are always being taken, lost, or trapped. Hollow herds, Sanguine raids, natural disasters.
5. **Moral Dilemmas** — No clean answers. Help the Covenant of Dusk establish blood tithes (saving lives but normalizing vampirism)? Or help The Salters burn Duskhollow to the ground (killing coexistence but eliminating a predator enclave)?
6. **Exploration** — The old world left ruins full of treasures and horrors. Malls, hospitals, military bases, mines.
7. **Personal** — Character-driven storylines about loss, identity, and what it means to stay human when humanity is an endangered species.

### The Breaks — Narrow Canyon

```
The canyon walls close in until your shoulders nearly brush both sides. Red
sandstone towers above you, striped with white and rust like the earth is
showing its ribs. The sky is a thin blue line directly overhead.

Water has carved this passage over millennia. Your boots splash in a trickle
```

### Duskhollow Manor — The Great Hall

```
Candlelight. Hundreds of candles, in iron candelabras and on every flat
surface, their flames barely moving in the still air. The great hall was once
a rancher's pride — vaulted timber ceiling, a stone fireplace large enough to
stand in, mounted elk heads watching from the walls with glass eyes that catch
the light.

The Covenant of Dusk has made it something else. Long tables draped in dark
cloth. Crystal glassware that predates the Collapse, filled with something too
dark and too thick to be wine. The smell is copper and woodsmoke and old roses.

Sanguine move between the tables with a grace that your hindbrain reads as
wrong. Too smooth. Too quiet. They're being polite. They're being civilized.
The part of you that evolved on the savanna is screaming anyway.

[Exits: north (kitchen), south (entrance hall), east (private chambers — RESTRICTED)]
```

## APPENDIX: WORLD RULES

1. **CHARON-7 is not magic.** Everything has a biological or scientific explanation, even if the characters don't understand it. The Sanguine are not supernatural vampires — they're a viral mutation. Keep it grounded.

2. **Sunlight weakens Sanguine but doesn't kill them.** Think severe migraine + sunburn + reduced stats. Not combustion. They can operate in daylight — they just hate it and are significantly less dangerous.

3. **There is no known cure.** Yet. Whether one exists or can be developed is a central mystery, not a foregone conclusion.

4. **The Hollow are not mindless.** They're *reduced*. Fragments of the person remain. This should be disturbing, not convenient.

5. **Silver does something to the Sanguine** and nobody knows why. It could be an allergic reaction to trace compounds. It could be psychosomatic. It works regardless. Don't over-explain it.

6. **Humanity is not doomed.** The Unturned are surviving. Communities are forming. Children are being born. The question isn't whether humanity survives — it's what kind of humanity survives.

7. **The Four Corners setting is real geography.** Use real landmarks, real terrain, real weather patterns. The San Juan Mountains, the Animas River, Mesa Verde, Shiprock — these places exist and should feel authentic to anyone who knows them.

8. **Player choice matters.** The faction reputation system should create meaningfully different experiences. A player beloved by The Accord and hated by The Red Court lives in a fundamentally different world than the reverse.
